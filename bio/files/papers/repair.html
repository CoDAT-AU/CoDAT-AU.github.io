Model and Program Repair via SAT Solvers

P.C. Attie K. Dak El Bab, and M. Sakr

We consider the subtractive model repair problem: given a finite Kripke structure M and a CTL formula f, determine if M contains a substructure M' that satisfies f. Thus, M can be 'repaired' to satisfy f by deleting some transitions and states. We map an instance (M, f) of model repair to a boolean formula repair(M, f) such that (M, f) has a solution iff repair(M, f) is satisfiable. Furthermore, a satisfying assignment determines which states and transitions must be removed from M to yield a model M' of f. Thus, we can use any SAT solver to repair Kripke structures. Using a complete SAT solver yields a complete algorithm: it always finds a repair if one exists. We also show that CTL model repair is NP-complete. We extend the basic repair method in three directions: (1) the use of abstraction mappings, i.e., repair a structure abstracted from M and then concretize the resulting repair to obtain a repair of M, (2) repair concurrent Kripke structures and concurrent programs: we use the pairwise method of Attie and Emerson to represent and repair the behavior of a concurrent program, as a set of 'concurrent Kripke structures', with only a quadratic increase in the size of the repair formula, and (3) repair hierarchical Kripke structures: we use a CTL formula to summarize the behavior of each 'box', and CTL deduction to relate the box formula with the overall specification.
